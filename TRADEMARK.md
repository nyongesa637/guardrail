# Guardrail Trademark Policy

> Last updated: 2026

"guardrail", the guardrail wordmark, and the guardrail mark
/ logo (the **guardrail Marks**) are trademarks of the
guardrail project. This policy explains what you may and may not
do with the guardrail Marks.

The AGPL-3.0 license under which this code is distributed grants
you broad rights to **the source code and binaries**. It does **not**
grant you any rights to the guardrail Marks. Trademark rights are
governed separately by this policy and by applicable trademark law.

## What you may do without asking

You may use the guardrail Marks to:

- **Refer to the project truthfully.** "I built X on top of
  guardrail" / "Compatible with guardrail" / "Forked from
  guardrail" — nominative fair use is fine.
- **Distribute unmodified copies** of guardrail under the
  project's name, with the original LICENSE, NOTICE, and TRADEMARK
  files preserved.
- **Show the mark in editorial / educational contexts** — articles,
  blog posts, conference talks, screenshots, video tutorials.
- **Link to the project** at https://github.com/nyongesa637/guardrail
  using the mark as a hyperlink.

In all cases the mark must remain unmodified — same colours, same
proportions, no recolouring or stretching — and you may not imply
endorsement or affiliation that doesn't exist.

## What requires prior written permission

You may **not** do the following without first obtaining written
permission from the project maintainer:

1. **Use the guardrail Marks to brand a fork.** If you modify
   guardrail and host it (or distribute a binary), pick a
   different name and mark for your distribution. You are free to
   write "based on guardrail" in the description, but the
   *product name* must be yours.
2. **Use the guardrail Marks for any commercial product or
   service** that is not the upstream guardrail project.
3. **Register a guardrail-related trademark or domain** in any
   jurisdiction.
4. **Combine the guardrail Marks with another mark** in a way
   that suggests a joint product (e.g. "guardrail + Acme").
5. **Use the guardrail Marks in a way that disparages the
   project, its contributors, or its users.**

## How to request permission

Open an issue prefixed with `[trademark]` describing:

- who you are,
- exactly how you want to use the guardrail Marks,
- a mock-up if relevant,
- and what jurisdiction.

A maintainer will reply within 30 days.

## What to do if you see misuse

If you spot someone using the guardrail Marks in a way this
policy forbids, please email the address listed in `SECURITY.md`
with a description and any evidence.
